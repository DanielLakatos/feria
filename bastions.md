# Bastions - Revision by Costa for Dungeons and Dragons 5th Edition (2014 Legacy Edition)

A Bastion is a location that belongs to a party of adventurers: a home, a stronghold, and a place of power that the party develops over the course of a campaign. A Bastion offers characters temporary refuge from the dangerous world of adventuring, and it provides opportunities for characters to craft magic items, conduct research, harvest poisons, build ships, and carry out a range of other activities.

There's no need to choose between going on adventures and commanding a Bastion; the party can do both at once. A Bastion has special facilities that generate benefits, and these facilities can also undertake projects while the party is otherwise occupied.

Most importantly, a Bastion is a creative playground for the players and a shared storytelling space in the campaign. Be as permissive as you can with the stories players tell in their Bastion, but players should know their control might be limited by the campaign's larger story, which you strive to make fun for everyone.

## Bastion Turns

As time passes in the campaign, players take Bastion turns to reflect the activity occurring in their Bastion, whether or not the characters are present. On a Bastion turn, the party can issue orders to the special facilities in their Bastion or issue the *Maintain* order to the entire Bastion (see "*Orders*").

### Frequency of Bastion Turns

By default, a Bastion turn occurs every 7 days of in-game time. Here are common examples of players taking one or more Bastion turns:

- The characters are on a long journey away from their Bastion. After the characters have been away for 7 days, you might say, "Time for a Bastion turn. Since you're not there, we'll assume you issue the *Maintain* order for your Bastion." Then roll for events as described in the "*Bastion Events*" section in this chapter.
- The characters spend 7 days or more in their Bastion between adventures. You might say, "You have six weeks to spend in your Bastion. Go ahead and resolve six Bastion turns."
- The characters return to their Bastion in the midst of an adventure. You might say, "You have just enough time to take a Bastion turn before you leave again in the morning."
- The characters are adventuring near their Bastion and resting in their Bastion at night. You might say, "It's been a week since your last Bastion turn, so you can take one now."

You can slow the frequency of Bastion turns to better serve the needs of your players and your campaign. For example, if the characters have months between adventures, you can call for a Bastion turn every month instead of every 7 days, so the characters aren't issuing so many orders or reaping too many benefits at once.

## Bastion Map

Encourage players to create floor plans of the party's Bastion, configuring its facilities as they see fit and using the same techniques you use to create dungeon maps (see "*Dungeons*" in *chapter 3* and "*Adventure Maps*" in *chapter 4*).

In addition to *basic* and *special facilities* (described later in this chapter), a Bastion can have the following features:

- **Closets.** A basic or special facility can have one or more closets, washrooms, or similar enclosures. The closets must be inside the facility and can't increase the facility's area in squares. These features are free.
- **Corridors, Ramps, and Staircases.** A facility can contain one or more corridors, ramps, or staircases leading to other facilities in the Bastion. These features are free.
- **Doors and Windows.** Each facility comes with one or more doors and shuttered windows, placed wherever the players see fit. See "*Doors*" in *chapter 3* for kinds of doors to choose from, including locked doors, secret doors, and portcullises. These features are free.
- **Defensive Walls.** The party can add defensive walls around their Bastion. A defensive wall is 20 feet high and may include a walkway along its top, with a means to access it (such as a ladder or lift). Each 5-foot square of defensive wall takes 10 days to build and costs 250 GP. If the party's Bastion is completely enclosed by defensive walls and it comes under attack (see "*Bastion Events*" at the end of this chapter), reduce by 2 the number of dice rolled to determine how many Bastion Defenders are lost in the attack.


### Facility Space

The amount of space in a basic or special facility determines its maximum area in 5-foot squares, as shown in the Facility Space table. The players can configure the squares of a facility as they please on the Bastion's map. The squares can be stacked so that a facility's area is distributed over multiple levels or stories.

##### Facility Space
| Space   | Maximum Area |
|---------|--------------|
| Cramped | 4 squares    |
| Roomy   | 16 squares   |
| Vast    | 36 squares   |

## Basic Facilities

The party's Bastion starts with free basic facilities, which the players choose from the Basic Facilities list below. A Bastion can have more than one of each basic facility.

#### Basic Facilities

- Bedroom (one for each player)
- Dining Room (amount of players divided by 5, minimum 1)
- Parlor (1)
- Courtyard (amount of players divided by 5, minimum 1)
- Kitchen (1)
- Storage (one Cramped storage for each player, storage for special Facilities are included)

A basic facility comes with nonmagical furnishings and decor appropriate for that facility.

Basic facilities don't have any game effects, but they can inspire roleplaying opportunities and enhance a Bastion's verisimilitude. A Bastion with a kitchen is functionally the same as one without, but the former gives you and your players a fun setting to start game sessions, have in-character discussions, or introduce new NPCs.

The party can add new basic facilities or enlarge existing ones by spending money and time, as discussed in the sections that follow. Any number of basic facilities can be added or enlarged at the same time. Party members do not need to be in their Bastion while basic facilities are being added or enlarged.

### Adding Basic Facilities

The party can add a basic facility to their Bastion by spending money and time. The cost of adding a basic facility and the time required depend on the facility's space, as shown on the table below.

| Facility Space |     Cost |   Time Required |
|----------------|---------:|----------------:|
| Cramped        |   500 GP | 2 bastion turns |
| Roomy          | 1,000 GP | 4 bastion turns |
| Vast           | 3,000 GP | 8 bastion turns |

### Enlarging Basic Facilities

There is no in-game benefit to enlarging a basic facility, but the party might enlarge a facility for cosmetic reasons or to increase the Bastion's size.

The party can spend money and time to increase the space of a basic facility in their Bastion by one category, as shown on the table below.

| Space Increase   |     Cost |         Time Required |
|------------------|---------:|----------------------:|
| Cramped to Roomy |   500 GP |       3 bastion turns |
| Roomy to Vast    | 2,000 GP |       6 bastion turns |

## Special Facilities

Special facilities are Bastion locations where certain activities yield game benefits. Initially, the Bastion has one special feature, a Planning Center. The players may choose to add more facilities up to a maximum based on the party's level. This limitation is added for balancing the game experience and preventing players from simply accumulating unlimited special facilities.

Unlike basic facilities, special facilities need blueprints or a special hireling to construct plus a gold and time cost. Blueprints can be acquired with adventuring and exploring the world, or during special research downtime activity. The Special Facility Amounts table shows the maximum number of special facilities in a Bastion.

##### Special Facility Amounts
| Level | Maximum Special Facilities |
|:-----:|:--------------------------:|
|   5   |              2             |
|   9   |              4             |
|   13  |              5             |
|   17  |              6             |

Special facilities have gold cost, required time to build and minimum room size differing based on the facility itself.

Deconstructing an existing special facility requires gold and time, shown in the table below. After the deconstruction is complete, the facility space is freed up for new special facilities.

| Facility Space |     Cost |   Time Required |
|----------------|---------:|----------------:|
| Cramped        |   250 GP | 1 bastion turns |
| Roomy          |   500 GP | 2 bastion turns |
| Vast           | 1,500 GP | 3 bastion turns |


### Requirements

Each special facility has a level requirement. At least one party member must be that level or higher to acquire that facility, regardless of having the blueprint. A special facility might also have a prerequisite that at least one party member must meet to gain that facility. For example, only a party with at least one member who can use an Arcane Focus or a tool as a Spellcasting Focus can have an *Arcane Study*.

### Space

A special facility occupies a certain amount of space (see "*Facility Space*"). The players can configure the squares of a special facility as desired on the Bastion's map. A special facility can be enlarged to grant additional benefits if its description says so.

### Hirelings

The players can assign names and personalities to hirelings in the party's Bastion using the same tools DMs use to *create NPCs* (see *chapter 3*).

Each special facility in a Bastion generates enough income to pay the salary of its hirelings. Hirelings follow the orders they're given and are loyal to the party.

### Orders

On a Bastion turn, the party can issue special orders—called Bastion orders—to one or more of the Bastion's special facilities. The party needn't issue orders to all the special facilities in their Bastion on a given Bastion turn.

The Maintain order is unusual; it is issued to the whole Bastion rather than to one or more special facilities. If the party is not in their Bastion on a given Bastion turn, the Bastion acts as though it were issued the Maintain order on that turn unless a party member can communicate with the Bastion hirelings using the *Sending* spell or similar magic.

The orders are as follows:

#### Craft

Hirelings in the special facility craft an item that can be made in that facility. During the time required to craft an item, the facility can't be used to craft anything else, even if a special ability allows the facility to carry out two orders at once. The hirelings have proficiencies with Artisan's Tools as specified in the facility's description.

#### Empower

The special facility confers a temporary empowerment to one or multiple party members or someone else.

#### Harvest

Hirelings gather a resource produced in the special facility. During the time required to harvest a resource, the facility can't be used to harvest anything else, even if a special ability allows the facility to carry out two orders at once.

#### Maintain

All the Bastion's hirelings focus on maintaining the Bastion rather than executing orders in special facilities. Issuing this order prohibits other orders from being issued to the Bastion on the current Bastion turn. Each time the Maintain order is issued, the DM rolls once on the *Bastion Events* table at the end of this chapter. Bastion events are resolved before the next Bastion turn.

#### Recruit

Hirelings recruit creatures to the Bastion. These creatures might include one or more Bastion Defenders, whose main purpose is to defend the Bastion if it is attacked (see "*Bastion Events*" at the end of this chapter). The Bastion generates enough income to provide for the needs of its Bastion Defenders.

#### Research

Hirelings in the special facility gather information.

#### Trade

Hirelings buy and sell goods or services stored or produced in this special facility.

## Bastion Events

At the end of each Bastion turn, the DM determines the Bastion's Event Roll Modifier based on its current status. The DM then rolls once on the Bastion Events table, applying this modifier to the result, to determine what event, if any, befalls the Bastion before the next Bastion Turn. If an event occurs, the DM reads the event aloud to the player whose character controls that Bastion. (All the events are described in the sections following the table.) The event is resolved immediately, with the player and DM working together to expand story details as needed. If multiple characters or Bastions are managed on the same Bastion turn, roll once on the table for each, resolving each event separately.

Bastion events reflect the ongoing life and challenges of a stronghold, whether the characters are present or away. When the Bastion's owner isn't present, these events can be opportunities for the player to take on the role of the Bastion's hirelings and roleplay their reactions to these events. The DM can even turn a Bastion event into a cutscene where each player takes on the role of one of the Bastion's hirelings.

### Bastion Event Roll Modifier

To introduce dynamic elements, the likelihood of certain events is influenced by the Bastion's current standing. Before rolling on the Bastion Events table, determine a **Bastion Event Roll Modifier** by assessing the Bastion's Reputation, Security, and Prosperity. Each category contributes a modifier based on its tier, as shown in the table below. Sum these modifiers to get the total modifier, which is then added to your 1d100 roll for the Bastion Event.

##### Bastion Status Modifiers
| Status Tier | Reputation | Security | Prosperity |
|:-----------:|:----------:|:--------:|:----------:|
| **Poor**    |    -5      |    -5    |    -5      |
| **Normal**  |     0      |     0    |     0      |
| **Good**    |    +5      |    +5    |    +5      |
| **Great**   |   +10      |   +10    |   +10      |

*DM Note: The DM determines the Bastion's current status in each category based on recent player actions, downtime activities, and ongoing campaign events. For example, building defensive walls improves Security, successfully completing trade orders boosts Prosperity, and resolving local quests enhances Reputation.*

##### Ambush

The Bastion has been targeted by a rival group or enemy faction. This event represents a small-scale ambush or a reconnaissance mission against the stronghold, designed to test its defenses, gather intelligence, or cause minor disruption. The nature of the encounter and whether it goes unnoticed or is discovered by your Bastion's defenders will be decided by the DM, tailored to your current situation. This event is also an opportune moment for the DM to introduce a new rival or advance an existing one's plans, contributing to a larger "attack meter" or escalating conflict against your Bastion.

##### Criminal Hireling

One of your Bastion's hirelings has a hidden past that has caught up with them. Whether a rogue with a bounty on their head, a runaway from a powerful organization, or a spy infiltrating your ranks, their secret has been exposed, drawing unwanted attention to your stronghold. The nature of this entanglement is revealed on a 1d6 roll:

| d6 |                               Outcome |
|:--:|:--------------------------------------|
| 1  | **Serious Offense.** A hireling has committed a significant crime. Local authorities arrive at your Bastion, demanding their arrest and trial. The party can choose to surrender the hireling or pay a fine of (character level / 2)d4 x 100 gold pieces to ensure their freedom and discreetly resolve the matter, risking a dip in local Reputation if word gets out. |
| 2  | **Minor Misdemeanor.** A hireling is wanted for a lesser offense. Local authorities seek their apprehension for a formal hearing. The party can either allow the hireling to be taken or pay a fine of (character level / 2)d4 x 50 gold pieces to settle the issue, potentially earning a small amount of goodwill if handled delicately. |
| 3  | **Cult's Vengeance.** A hireling escaped a dangerous cult, and the cultists have tracked them to your Bastion. A surprise ambush on the Bastion ensues, requiring a quick resolution with a few dice rolls to determine the immediate outcome and whether the hireling can escape or must be defended. |
| 4  | **Personal Rivalry.** A hireling's personal rival has discovered their location. The rival acts unexpectedly, perhaps posing as a new hireling, a visiting guest, or even a local merchant to gain access and plot their revenge or abduction from within. The DM will decide the rival's exact strategy, leading to a tense social encounter or a subtle threat. |
| 5  | **Guild Dispute.** A hireling is entangled in a guild dispute and is formally required to appear for its resolution. The party might be drawn into intricate guild politics, needing to mediate, provide protection, or even represent their hireling's interests, potentially gaining powerful allies or making new enemies within the region's influential guilds. |
| 6  | **Undercover Agent.** A seemingly minor hireling is, in fact, an undercover spy for a rival faction or enemy organization. Roll a d20: on a 10 or higher, they are discovered in the act of sabotage or espionage within the Bastion, leading to a confrontation. On a 9 or lower, their actions go unnoticed for now, but a special facility might suffer a setback (e.g., temporary shutdown, lost resources) or the party's rivals gain a strategic advantage in a future encounter. |

##### Lost Hirelings

A situation at the Bastion, or personal circumstances, leads to one or more hirelings becoming lost or choosing to depart from your service. This could be due to a recent order in a special facility going awry, an unfulfilled promise, a hireling pursuing a new opportunity, or simply the call of a different life. The specific reasons and the number of hirelings affected will be determined by the DM.

##### Facility Breakdown

One or more of your Bastion's facilities suffers a critical malfunction or structural damage, rendering it temporarily unusable. The cause could range from magical interference, sabotage by a rival, a disastrous mishap during a facility's operation, or unforeseen structural stress. The DM will determine the specific reason, creating a narrative opportunity.

To determine the extent and duration of the breakdown, and if the party's intervention is required, roll as follows:

1.  **Number of Facilities Affected:** Roll 1d6.
    *   **1-3:** One facility is affected.
    *   **4-5:** Two facilities are affected.
    *   **6:** All special facilities become temporarily unusable.
2.  **Duration of Breakdown:** Roll 1d4.
    *   **1-3:** The affected facilities are unusable for 1 Bastion turn.
    *   **4:** The affected facilities are unusable for 2 Bastion turns.
3.  **Party Input Required:** Roll 1d6.
    *   **1:** **Financial Strain.** The party must pay gold equal to half the total construction cost of the affected facilities to cover repairs and specialized materials. If they cannot or choose not to, the facilities remain broken for three times the determined duration.
    *   **2:** **Personal Attention.** All affected facilities remain broken until at least one party member physically returns to the Bastion to oversee or personally conduct repairs. They are needed to be present for an entire bastion turn.
    *   **3:** **External Threat.** The party or their trusted hirelings must deal with an external situation, such as disabling nearby siege equipment set up by enemies, rescuing captured specialists, or retrieving crucial components from a dangerous location.
    *   **4-6:** **Self-Contained.** No direct party input is immediately needed. The Bastion's hirelings can resolve the issue, though this might consume resources or slow other projects.

##### Unexpected Expenses

An unforeseen financial demand arises within your Bastion, possibly stemming from a trade deal gone awry, a crafting project encountering rare material shortages, or a critical order requiring specialized components. This sudden need for funds creates a financial pinch, requiring an immediate or impending investment.

The amount of gold needed is (character level)d4, with the multiplier determined by the DM to represent the scale of the expense (e.g., x100 for a major setback, x50 for a significant cost, x25 for a moderate expenditure). This gold must either be drawn from the Bastion's coffers or supplied by the party members within 1d4 Bastion turns. Failure to meet this demand could result in delayed projects, lost resources, or a hit to the Bastion's Prosperity.

##### Local Rivalry

The Bastion finds itself embroiled in a subtle, yet persistent, rivalry with a local faction or burgeoning enemy. Unlike an outright ambush, this event represents less direct actions—scheming, undermining, or accumulating advantages that might not be immediately visible to the party. These actions sow the seeds for future conflict, eroding your Bastion's standing or empowering your adversaries.

The DM will determine the specific manifestation of this rivalry, or roll a d20:

*   **1-4:** Rivals gain a strategic advantage, potentially increasing the number of combatants or resources they'll have in a future direct confrontation.
*   **5-14:** The chance of an attack on your Bastion increases by 1d2.
*   **15-19:** Rivals successfully disrupt a trade route or resource supply, imposing a -1 penalty on a future Prosperity check or adding 100gp to the cost of the next Trade or Craft order.
*   **20:** The rivals' preparations culminate, granting them the ability to initiate a full-scale attack on your Bastion as early as the next Bastion turn, at the DM's discretion. This event serves as a clear warning of impending danger.

##### Request for Aid

A local group, community, or even a neighboring settlement sends an urgent plea for assistance to your Bastion. This could manifest as a nearby town requiring immediate supplies, a village under attack needing armed defenders, or a pressing crisis that demands the unique capabilities of your stronghold and its inhabitants.

If the party members are present in the Bastion, or if they can be swiftly contacted, they must decide whether to answer this call. Their choice will lead to varying outcomes, shaped by the DM to fit the ongoing narrative. Refusing aid might result in a temporary or even permanent decline in the Bastion's Reputation with the affected group and the surrounding area. Conversely, offering assistance could yield valuable rewards, open new opportunities, or forge powerful alliances in the future.

Here are some examples of urgent requests for aid:

| 1d5 | Event |
|:---:|-------|
| 1 | **Urgent Supply Run**: A nearby town's granary was destroyed by fire, or its water source was contaminated. They desperately need the Bastion to dispatch immediate food, clean water, or specialized supplies to prevent famine or illness. |
| 2 | **Impending Attack**: A scout or messenger arrives, breathless, reporting a monstrous horde (goblins, orcs, undead, etc.) or a bandit gang marching towards a defenseless village or a crucial strategic point. They need the Bastion's defenders to reinforce their meager forces immediately. |
| 3 | **Magical Emergency**: A localized magical phenomenon (a wild magic zone, a rampaging elemental, a spreading blight) threatens to engulf a populated area or vital resource. The Bastion's arcane specialists or magical resources are specifically requested to contain or counteract it. |
| 4 | **Hostage Situation**: A prominent figure, a vital merchant, or a group of innocent travelers has been taken hostage by brigands or monstrous humanoids within a short distance of the Bastion. Immediate intervention is required to save them before it's too late. |
| 5 | **Critical Infrastructure Failure**: A vital bridge, a dam, or a mine shaft has collapsed due to an earthquake or sabotage, cutting off trade or threatening a large area. The Bastion's engineers, laborers, or even specific magical abilities are needed for immediate, critical repairs. |

##### Refugees

A desperate group of wandering travelers arrives at your Bastion's gates, seeking temporary shelter. These refugees might be honest folk fleeing hardship, displaced villagers seeking safety, or humble beggars driven by hunger and desperation. Their plight presents a poignant test of your Bastion's compassion and resources.

The DM will determine the duration of their stay and their true nature. Welcoming these weary souls might significantly boost your Bastion's Reputation among local communities, and their gratitude could manifest as future compensation or valuable connections. However, caution is advised, for among them could lurk cunning spies sent by a rival faction, or even bandits in disguise, waiting for an opportune moment to betray your trust and launch an ambush during a subsequent Bastion turn.












## Attack on the Bastion

The party's Bastion is more than just a home; it's a statement, a growing power in the world. As such, it naturally attracts attention – some positive, some undeniably hostile. The **Attack on the Bastion** subsystem ensures that your stronghold becomes a dynamic part of the ongoing campaign, a focal point where world events, player choices, and rival ambitions collide. This system aims for a dramatic back-and-forth, building tension and rewarding strategic thinking, culminating in epic confrontations that affect the Bastion's fate. It is up to theDM to decide if they want to use this subsystem as for some parties it may not be the right fit for their campaign.

### Establishing a Rival

The first step in any Bastion attack is the emergence of a **Rival**. A Rival is a significant NPC or faction that views the party's Bastion, or the party themselves, as a threat, an obstacle, or a valuable target. Rivals are deeply rooted in your campaign's narrative and should make sense within the established story and world.

#### DM Guidance: Choosing a Rival

*   **Story Integration:** A Rival can emerge naturally from the campaign's plot. Perhaps the party's Bastion encroaches on a powerful creature's territory, or their growing influence challenges a local lord.
*   **Bastion Events:** Sometimes, a Bastion Event (such as *Ambush* or *Local Rivalry*) might be the seed from which a Rival grows, escalating minor skirmishes into a full-blown threat.
*   **Archetypes:** Think about classic antagonist archetypes to give the Rival understandable motivations and methods:
    *   **The Undead Lich:** Eyes the Bastion as a new, formidable lair. Their attacks might involve necromantic incursions, attempts to corrupt defenders, or siege warfare with undead hordes.
    *   **Local Authorities:** View the party's presence as a destabilizing force or a challenge to their power. Their attacks could be legal injunctions, arrests of hirelings, or organized military sieges.
    *   **Rival Adventuring Party/Guild:** Sees the Bastion's resources, fame, or territory as something they can claim. Their tactics might involve direct assaults, subterfuge, or even attempts to sabotage special facilities.
    *   **Monstrous Horde:** Orcs, goblins, giants, or other intelligent monsters might see the Bastion as a source of plunder or a strategic location to claim. Their attacks will likely be direct, brutal, and focused on overwhelming defenses.
    *   **Thieves' Guild/Assassins:** Seek specific items, information, or wish to eliminate the party's influence. Their attacks are likely stealthy, focusing on infiltration, ambushes, and targeting key personnel or facilities.

Once a Rival is established, the **Chance of Attack** begins to track their growing hostility.

### Chance of Attack

The **Chance of Attack** is a numerical value, starting at 0, that represents the Rival's readiness and opportunity to launch an assault on the Bastion. This value fluctuates based on the campaign's flow, player actions, and random events, creating an escalating sense of tension.

#### How it Works

On each **Bastion turn**, after all orders are issued and Bastion Events resolved, roll a `d20` to check the Rival's activity:

1.  **Attack Triggers:**
    *   If the `d20` roll is **less than or equal to the current Chance of Attack**, an **Attack on the Bastion** occurs. The DM decides whether the attack unfolds during the current Bastion turn or the very next, based on what makes narrative sense. Based DM choice, a successful attack may instead be used as an opportunity for the Rival to gain a strategic advantage and advance their progress, while the player party understands a large amount of progress is made by their rivals. The DM may involve a system where the **Attack Strength** is a separate tracker from the chance of attack, and each successful attack trigger is up to the DM to decide if its an attack on the bastion or the strength of the attack is increased by 1.
    *   If the `d20` roll is **less than or equal to (Chance of Attack + DM Modifier)**, the Chance of Attack increases by 1. The **DM Modifier** can be +5, +10, or another number the DM deems appropriate for the party's level and the campaign's pacing. This represents the Rival testing defenses or gaining confidence.
2.  **No Immediate Event:** In all other cases, nothing happens this Bastion turn regarding this Rival's attack readiness.

#### Modifying the Chance of Attack

The Chance of Attack is a dynamic slider, influenced by various factors:

*   **Player Actions:** Strategic choices, such as reinforcing defenses, performing counter-reconnaissance, or directly confronting the Rival's lieutenants, can decrease the Chance of Attack. Ignoring threats or provoking the Rival could increase it.
*   **World Events:** Political shifts, natural disasters, or other major campaign developments can shift the Rival's priorities, either raising or lowering their immediate threat level.
*   **Bastion Events:** Specific Bastion Events (e.g., *Ambush*, *Local Rivalry*) can explicitly state that they increase or decrease the Chance of Attack, reflecting the Rival's probing actions.
*   **DM Discretion:** The DM can adjust the Chance of Attack at any time to serve the narrative, increase tension, or reflect the Rival's evolving plans.

### The Attack

When an Attack on the Bastion is triggered, the specific nature of the assault is determined by the Rival's capabilities, their objective, and crucially, whether the Player Characters are present.

#### Nature of the Encounter

The chosen Rival archetype dictates the style of the attack:

*   **Orc Hordes:** Expect a brutal, direct siege focused on breaching walls and overwhelming defenders in the courtyard or at the gates.
*   **Thieves' Guild:** Anticipate a stealthy infiltration, perhaps during the night, targeting specific hirelings, sleeping characters, or storage facilities. The combat might be smaller scale, focused on ambushes and quick objectives.
*   **Lich's Army:** Could involve a frontal assault with undead minions, magical bombardments, or even incorporeal entities phasing through defenses. The target might be a powerful artifact or the Bastion's magical wards.
*   **Rival Adventurers:** May employ cunning tactics, feints, or focus on disabling specific special facilities before a direct confrontation.

#### Attack Objective

Most Rivals have a goal beyond simple destruction. This objective shapes the encounter and provides the party with a clear defense strategy:

*   **Territory Claim:** The Rival wants to occupy the Bastion. The objective is to defeat all defenders and secure key areas.
*   **Specific Items:** A powerful magic item, a rare crafting recipe, or a store of valuable resources. The Rival will focus on the facility or chamber housing this item.
*   **Elimination:** The Rival seeks to eradicate the party or their key hirelings. Attacks might target specific bedrooms or critical command centers.
*   **Disruption:** The Rival aims to disable special facilities, destroy food supplies, or spread chaos.
*   **Intelligence:** The Rival might seek to kidnap hirelings for information or steal crucial documents.

#### Attack Strength

The current **Chance of Attack** value provides guidance for the DM on the overall strength and scale of the assault. Or if DM chooses to use a separate tracker they may use the **Attack Strength** value instead. Then Attack Strength starts at 0 and increases with each successful trigger chosen to increase the attack strength.

*   **1. Low Power Attack** (Low Chance of Attack (1-5)): A smaller-scale probing attack. Perhaps a handful of scouts, a minor magical intrusion, or a single assassin. The Bastion's defenses are likely to hold, but the Rival gains intel.
*   **2. Moderate Power Attack** (Chance of Attack 6-10): A more substantial assault. A small war band, a focused team of infiltrators, or a minor siege attempt. Damage to defenses or facilities is possible, and the Bastion's defenders will be significantly tested.
*   **3. High Power Attack** (Chance of Attack 11+): A full-blown offensive. A large force, potent magical support, or highly skilled special units. The Bastion will face a serious challenge, and initial breaches or successful objectives by the Rival are highly probable.

### Party Presence

The most significant factor in resolving an attack is whether the player characters are present in the Bastion or can arrive at the bastion from a remote location.

*   **Party Present:** If the characters are physically in the Bastion when the attack occurs, this becomes a full-scale combat encounter, potentially evolving into a multi-stage challenge.
    *   **DM Guidance:** Design the encounter using standard combat rules. The deadliness of the encounter should be appropriate for the party's level, but remember the Bastion's goal is to be a haven. While challenging, outright TPKs due to a Bastion attack should be rare unless the party has grossly underestimated the threat or the narrative specifically demands it. The Bastion's defensive walls, Bastion Defenders, and special facility actions can provide significant aid.
*   **Party Absent:** If the characters are away, the attack must be resolved differently.
    *   **Hireling Resolution:** The players can roleplay as their Bastion's key hirelings, making strategic decisions during the attack. This allows for player engagement without the main characters. The DM can run a simplified combat or skill challenge based on the hirelings' abilities and numbers.
    *   **Simulated Auto-Battle:** If the players prefer, the attack can be resolved quickly using dice rolls based on the Bastion's defenses, the number of Bastion Defenders, and the Rival's capabilities.
        *   **DM Guidance:** While auto-resolve should favor the Rival more often than not (representing the PCs' absence), it should **never** result in the instant, complete destruction of the Bastion. The goal is to create consequences and narrative hooks, not frustration.

## Resolving the Attack: Outcomes and Consequences

After the attack, the DM determines its outcome based on whether the Rival achieved their objective, how well the Bastion's defenses held, and the overall narrative of the encounter. This outcome then dictates the consequences for the Bastion and the future trajectory of the Rival's threat.

### Attack Outcome Criteria

*   **Successful Attack (for Rival):** The Rival achieved their primary objective (e.g., stole the item, disabled the facility, kidnapped a hireling), even if they suffered heavy losses or were ultimately driven off.
*   **Stalemate:** Neither side fully achieved their goals. The Rival might have caused some damage or minor disruption, but failed to secure their main objective. The Bastion held, but at a significant cost.
*   **Failed Attack (for Rival):** The Rival was decisively repelled and failed to achieve any significant objective. They might have suffered heavy losses or been forced to retreat quickly.

### Post-Attack Consequences

Once the DM determines the outcome, use the table below to apply the appropriate consequences. After a successful attack, a stalemate, or a non-critical failure, the Rival needs time to regroup or plan their next move. This is represented by a **Cooldown** period, during which the Chance of Attack cannot increase or trigger another attack.

## Multiple Rivals (Optional Rule)

For campaigns where the Bastion plays a very central role and players enjoy complex strategic management, the DM may choose to track multiple Rivals simultaneously. Each Rival would have its own independent **Chance of Attack** score, and potentially different objectives or attack styles. This can lead to engaging scenarios where the party must prioritize defenses or even leverage one Rival against another. This option adds significant complexity and should be introduced at the DM's discretion, ensuring it enhances rather than overwhelms the gameplay experience.
